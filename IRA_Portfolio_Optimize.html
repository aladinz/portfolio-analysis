What Was Eliminated and Why
✕ QUAL — Eliminated (11% → 0%)
At 0.93 correlation with VTI, QUAL is statistically a more expensive, less diversified version of VTI's top tier. Its top holdings — Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, Meta, Alphabet — are nearly identical to VTI's top holdings. The 11% allocation added meaningful concentration without adding a materially different return stream. The 11% is redeployed into IAU and VTIP, the two assets that cover the regime (inflation/stagflation) most likely to destroy a QUAL-heavy portfolio.
✕ AVGO — Eliminated (4% → 0%)
True effective AVGO weight was ~5.1% across four layers: direct (4%) + XLK (~0.6%) + QUAL (~0.3%) + VTI (~0.2%). Holding it directly added single-stock earnings event risk on top of an already concentrated underlying position. The semiconductor thesis is fully expressed through VTI's inherent tech weight and XLK.
✕ VRT — Eliminated (3% → 0%)
The AI data center power/cooling thesis is valid, but 3% is too small to materially move the portfolio. It adds single-stock monitoring cost for roughly 0.3% tracking difference against XLK. Note: VRT can return as an optional 9th position at 4–5% (funded by trimming VTI) if this remains a high-conviction individual name.
✕ AMZN — Eliminated (2% → 0%)
At 2%, AMZN cannot move the portfolio. AMZN comprises ~3.5% of VTI, giving ~0.7% effective exposure through the core already. The direct allocation adds earnings-monitoring overhead for a position too small to generate meaningful alpha contribution.
