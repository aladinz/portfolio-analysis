Key Advisory Decisions

The three judgment calls made during the process

Decision 1 — BNDW Eliminated in Optimize

The /optimize pass removed BNDW entirely because it directly contradicted the "rates higher for longer" belief. In isolation, this was the logically correct call for a conviction portfolio.

Decision 2 — User Re-introduced BNDW

User's instinct to keep a rate-downside hedge was sound — the rate path is genuinely uncertain. The error was proposing BNDW at 15% vs VTIP at 10%, which inverted the primary/hedge relationship.

Decision 3 — BNDW Demoted to 12% Hedge

Agreed to BNDW 12%, VTIP 12% — equal weight. Not ideal (VTIP should lead for belief alignment) but reflects genuine uncertainty. BNDW's role is explicit: rate-cut insurance, not a thesis expression.

Remaining Optional Improvement

BNDW 12%→7%, VTIP 12%→17% would push the grade from B to B+. Only 2 rebalancing trades. Execute if "rates higher for longer" conviction strengthens.

What to Watch

Conditions that should trigger a rebalance or position review

01
Fed signals a rate-cut cycle (2+ cuts priced in)
Action: Increase BNDW from 12%→18%, reduce VTIP from 12%→8%. The rate-path uncertainty shifts — BNDW becomes a conviction position rather than a hedge. PDBC may also soften in a disinflationary rate-cut environment.
02
Inflation re-accelerates above 4%
Action: Trim BNDW from 12%→5–7%, increase VTIP toward 17–18%. Conviction on Beliefs #1 and #3 is confirmed. PDBC may also warrant a bump toward 10–12%.
03
XLK underperforms VTI by >15% over 12 months
Action: Review the tech leadership belief. If the divergence is structural (not cyclical), reducing XLK from 10%→5% and reallocating to VXUS or XLV would be appropriate. The belief is the reason XLK exists in the portfolio.
04
Commodity cycle peaks (DBC/PDBC –30%+ from highs)
Action: Trim PDBC from 8%→4%, redistribute to VTI or VTIP. The commodity belief has been expressed and partially realized. Chasing a declining commodity cycle is outside the profile's risk tolerance.
05
Annual rebalancing — bands approach
Rule: Rebalance annually or when any position drifts ±5pp from target (e.g., VTI rises to 33%+ or falls below 23%). Low-maintenance profile means no intra-year tactical moves unless a watch condition above is triggered.
